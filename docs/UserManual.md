# User Manual

## Purpose

This system is an ERP platform for running day-to-day business operations in one place. It helps staff manage sales, purchasing, inventory, reports, administration, and audit history without needing separate tools for each process.

Use it to:

- keep customer, supplier, and product records in one system
- track stock levels and warehouse movements
- create sales orders, invoices, purchase orders, and supplier invoices
- record payments and monitor outstanding balances
- review dashboards, reports, and audit history
- manage users and access rights if you are an administrator

## Who Uses It

Different staff members will usually use different parts of the system:

- Sales staff handle customers, sales orders, invoices, and payments
- Purchasing staff handle suppliers, purchase orders, and goods receipts
- Store or inventory staff track stock, transfers, and adjustments
- Managers review dashboards and reports
- Administrators manage users, roles, and system settings

## Getting Started

### Sign in

1. Open the ERP website in your browser.
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Select Sign In.

If your login is accepted, you will see the main dashboard.

### If you cannot sign in

- Check that your email and password are correct.
- If you enter the wrong password too many times, your account may be locked temporarily.
- If you still cannot sign in, contact an administrator.

## Main Areas of the System

### Dashboard

The dashboard gives a quick summary of the business. It may show things like:

- stock value
- low-stock items
- sales this month
- open sales and purchase orders
- amounts owed by customers and to suppliers

Use the dashboard first when you want a quick picture of business activity.

### Products

Use Products to store the items your business buys and sells.

You can:

- add new products
- update product details
- set categories and units of measure
- manage pricing and tax-related information

Keep product records accurate because they are used in sales, purchasing, inventory, and reporting.

### Customers

Use Customers to store customer details for sales.

You can:

- create a new customer
- update contact details
- use customer records when creating sales orders and invoices

### Suppliers

Use Suppliers to store vendor or supplier details for purchasing.

You can:

- create a supplier record
- update supplier contact details
- use supplier records when creating purchase orders and supplier invoices

### Inventory

Use Inventory to track stock in warehouses.

You can:

- see stock levels
- receive stock
- issue stock out
- transfer stock between warehouses
- make stock adjustments after stock-take

The system keeps stock history so you can see how inventory changed over time.

### Sales Orders

Use Sales Orders when you want to record a customer order before invoicing.

Typical flow:

1. Create the sales order.
2. Confirm the order.
3. Fulfil the order when stock is ready to ship or deliver.
4. Create an invoice from the order.
5. Issue the invoice.
6. Record payments as they are received.

### Invoices

Use Invoices to bill customers for goods or services.

You can:

- view invoice totals
- issue invoices
- record part payments or full payments
- check whether an invoice is open, partially paid, or fully paid

### Purchase Orders

Use Purchase Orders when buying goods from suppliers.

Typical flow:

1. Create a purchase order.
2. Confirm the order.
3. Record goods receipts when items arrive.
4. Create a supplier invoice from the order.
5. Approve the supplier invoice.
6. Record supplier payments.

### Reports

Use Reports to review business information and export it when needed.

Examples include:

- inventory valuation
- business summaries
- operational reports

Reports may be downloadable in formats such as CSV, Excel, or PDF.

### Audit

Use Audit to review important changes made in the system.

This is useful when you want to know:

- who changed a record
- what changed
- when the change happened

Access to audit history is usually restricted to administrators.

### Administration

Use Administration if you manage users and system setup.

You can typically:

- create or manage user accounts
- assign roles and permissions
- update business settings and reference data

## Common Workflows

### Adding a new customer

1. Open Customers.
2. Select Add New.
3. Enter the customer name and contact details.
4. Save the record.

### Creating a sales order

1. Open Sales Orders.
2. Select Create Order.
3. Choose the customer.
4. Add the products and quantities.
5. Save the order.
6. Confirm the order when it is ready.

### Issuing a customer invoice

1. Open the sales order or invoice area.
2. Create an invoice from the order if needed.
3. Check the quantities, prices, and tax details.
4. Issue the invoice.
5. Record payment when the customer pays.

### Receiving goods from a supplier

1. Open Purchase Orders.
2. Select the confirmed purchase order.
3. Enter the received quantities.
4. Save the goods receipt.
5. Check that stock has increased in Inventory.

### Creating a supplier invoice

1. Open Supplier Invoices.
2. Create an invoice from the purchase order if required.
3. Review the supplier amounts.
4. Approve the invoice.
5. Record the payment when it is made.

### Transferring stock

1. Open Inventory.
2. Select Transfer.
3. Choose the product, source warehouse, and destination warehouse.
4. Enter the quantity.
5. Save the transfer.

### Adjusting stock after a stock count

1. Open Inventory.
2. Select Adjust.
3. Enter the counted quantity.
4. Add a reason for the adjustment.
5. Save the change.

## What the Statuses Mean

You may see different statuses while working:

- Draft: the record has been started but not yet confirmed
- Confirmed: the record has been approved for the next step
- Fulfilled: the sales order has been completed from stock
- Received: the purchase order goods have arrived
- Partially received or partially paid: only part of the total has been completed
- Issued: an invoice has been finalized and sent into the process
- Voided or canceled: the record was closed and will not continue

If you are unsure which status to use, ask a manager before changing a live document.

## Best Practices

- Enter records as soon as the business event happens.
- Keep product, customer, and supplier details accurate.
- Check quantities carefully before confirming orders or posting stock movements.
- Use the correct warehouse when receiving, issuing, or transferring stock.
- Add reasons when adjusting stock.
- Review reports regularly so issues are caught early.
- Use audit history if you need to trace a change.

## Common Problems

### I cannot see a record I expect

- Check your filters and search terms.
- Make sure the record was saved.
- Confirm you are in the correct module.

### A button is not available

- Your account may not have permission.
- The document may need to move to a previous step first.
- Some actions are only available to administrators or managers.

### A stock action failed

- Check that enough stock is available.
- Check that you selected the correct warehouse.
- Review the quantities before trying again.

### The numbers do not look right

- Refresh the page and check the latest record.
- Review the related order, receipt, or invoice.
- Contact a supervisor if the issue remains.

## Simple Glossary

- ERP: a system that combines several business functions in one application
- Inventory: goods you keep in stock
- Purchase order: a request to buy goods from a supplier
- Sales order: a request from a customer to buy goods
- Invoice: a bill for goods or services
- Goods receipt: confirmation that supplier goods arrived
- Audit log: a history of changes made in the system
